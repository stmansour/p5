The Game of Life is a simulation of the life of a cell in a grid of cells. Each cell can be in one of two possible states, live or dead (think: populated and unpopulated). Live cells are shown in blue, dead cells are white. The grid is initialize randomly, the random seed is shown and can be used to regenerate the simluation. Whether a cell survives to the next generation depends on its neigbors:

  1. Any live cell with two or three live neighbors survives.
  2. Any dead cell with three live neighbors becomes a live cell.
  3. All other live cells die in the next generation. Similarly, all other dead cells stay dead.

This code continuously calculates next generations. Each animation frame shows a new generation. Over time the generations tend to stabilize, meaning that generations begin to repeat over some number of generations. This code currently checks for stable generations over a 15 generation period. If the repeat cyle occurs over more than 15 generations it will not be detected. Currently, I have not seen anything stabilize over more than 6 generations. But it could happen.
